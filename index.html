About Yankari Game Reserve

Yankari Game Reserve is a large wildlife park located in the south-central part of Bauchi State, in northeastern Nigeria. It covers an area of about 2,244 km2 (866 sq mi) with an elevation of about 1,600 feet (500 m) and has characteristic savanna vegetation, including swamps in river floodplains, grasslands, and thick bush. It is home to several natural warm water springs The entire park is based on the Tertiary-aged Kerri Formation, which is made of sandstone, siltstones, kaolinites, and grits.

The open country and villages that surround Yankari National Park are populated by farmers and herders, but there has been no human settlement in the park for over a century. There is, however, evidence of earlier human habitation in the park, including old iron smelting sites and caves. There are ancient sandstone cisterns, carved by former inhabitants for water storage. The furnaces have been damaged by centuries of exposure to the elements, though by the late 1990s there were more than fifty surviving in the Delimiri and Ampara area.

Frequently asked questions

What do I need to pack for a safari resort?

You should pack light, comfortable clothing in neutral colors, a hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, insect repellent, binoculars, camera, charger, adapter, and any personal medication. You may also need a warm jacket or fleece for the evenings and early mornings, as well as a raincoat or poncho for the rainy season.

What is the climate like at a safari resort?

The climate varies depending on the location and season of your safari resort. Generally, the dry season (May to October) is cooler and less humid, while the wet season (November to April) is warmer and more humid. The average temperature ranges from 15°C to 35°C throughout the year.

What are the safety and health precautions to take at a safari resort?

You should consult your doctor or travel clinic before traveling to a safari resort, as you may need vaccinations or prophylaxis for malaria and other diseases. You should also follow the advice and instructions of your safari guide and staff, and respect the wildlife and environment. You should avoid drinking tap water and eating raw or uncooked food, and carry a first aid kit and bottled water with you.

What are the activities and experiences that a safari resort offers?

A safari resort offers a variety of activities and experiences to suit your preferences and interests. You can enjoy game drives, bush walks, birdwatching, cultural visits, scenic flights, hot air balloon rides, canoeing, fishing, and more. You can also relax and unwind at the spa, pool, or lounge of your safari resort, and enjoy the delicious cuisine and drinks.

How much does a safari resort cost and what is included in the price?

The cost of a safari resort depends on the location, season, duration, accommodation, and activities of your safari. You can expect to pay anywhere from $200 to $2000 per person per night, depending on the level of luxury and exclusivity you choose. The price usually includes accommodation, meals, drinks, game drives, park fees, and transfers. Some safari resorts may also include flights, laundry, gratuities, and other activities in the price.

How do I book a safari resort?

You can book a safari resort through a reputable travel agent or tour operator, who can help you plan and customize your safari according to your budget, preferences, and needs. You can also book directly with the safari resort of your choice, if they have online booking facilities. You should book well in advance, especially during peak seasons, to secure your spot and get the best deals.

What is the best time to visit a safari resort?

The best time to visit a safari resort depends on what you want to see and do. Generally, the dry season (May to October) is the best time for wildlife viewing, as the animals congregate around water sources and the vegetation is sparse. The wet season (November to April) is the best time for birdwatching, as many migratory birds arrive and the landscape is green and lush. However, each season has its own advantages and disadvantages, and you can have a great safari experience at any time of the year.

Is a safari resort kid friendly and handicap accessible?

Most safari resorts are kid friendly and handicap accessible, but you should check with your travel agent or the safari resort before booking. Some safari resorts may have age restrictions, special facilities, or activities for children, while others may not. Some safari resorts may also have wheelchair access, ramps, or lifts, while others may not. You should also consider the physical demands and safety aspects of a safari, and choose a safari resort that suits your abilities and needs.

What about the pickup and drop off after the safari resort?

Most safari resorts provide pickup and drop off services from the nearest airport or town, either by road or by air. You should confirm the details and timings of your pickup and drop off with your travel agent or the safari resort before your arrival and departure. You should also have your passport, visa, and travel documents ready, and follow the luggage and weight restrictions of your safari resort.

How long is a safari resort?

A safari resort can be as long or as short as you want, depending on your budget, preferences, and availability. You can choose to stay at one safari resort for the entire duration of your safari, or you can combine different safari resorts in different locations and regions. The average length of a safari resort is 7 to 10 days, but you can also opt for shorter or longer safaris. You should also consider the travel time and distance between your safari resort and your arrival and departure points.
